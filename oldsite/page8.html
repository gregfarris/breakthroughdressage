Misty Meadow Farm boasts outstanding and well maintained facilities on 70 beautiful acres. It is located is Parma, Michigan, two miles south of I-94, just west of Jackson, Michigan. Misty Meadow is the dressage training barn of USDF bronze and silver medalist Jessie Myers. The barn contains 22 roomy and well ventilated rubber-matted stalls, a lighted 60' X 150' indoor riding arena with excellent footing and mirrored walls, an indoor wash rack, and wide well-lighted aisles with multiple cross ties and electrical outlets. The grounds include a lighted 80' X 200' outdoor arena, numerous spacious paddocks, and large open fields and woods for hacking out.

Board at Misty Meadow Farm is a flat rate of $375/month per horse for stall board and $225/month per horse for a paddock with shed. Stall boarders receive daily turnout, either individually or with a buddy, and the changing of blankets and bell boots as needed. All horses are given grain and hay morning and evening, as well as lunch hay. Owner-provided supplements are given with the evening grain.

For the rider, the farm offers a heated tackroom and bathroom, a comfortable observation room above the arena, large individual storage cabinets, and perhaps most importantly, a relaxed and friendly atmosphere in which to enjoy and learn with your horse.

Stalls are available. For information, please use "Contact Jessie" button to send message to Jessie. Or, call her at 734-730-5181.

Overview
 

Facilities: 22 Stalls; Indoor Arena; Outdoor Arena; Turnout Paddocks; Viewing Area; Indoor Wash Rack; Heated Tack Room; Heated Bathroom; Individual Lockers for Boarders; Lights for Night Riding; Grounds and trails for hacking.

Horse Breed Specialization: Arabian; Lipizzan; Warmblood; all breeds welcome.

Boarding: Full Service: $375/month for stall board and $225/month for paddock board. Includes: 3 feedings/day, rubber matted box stall cleaned daily, daily turnout individually or in pairs, blanket changes, boots for turnout, use of all areas of facility.

Extra Boarding Services: worming, specialized feeding programs, and grooming services available for additional fee.

Lessons: Private.  See fee Schedule.

School horses available: Yes

Lesson types: Dressage

Instructor: Jessie Myers of Breakthrough Dressage

Horse Shows: While we do not hold horse shows at the farm, students may attend 6-8 USDF recognized competitions annually. Novice students and horses are able to get out to 2 or 3 local schooling shows each year.

Transportation: Can be provided to shows and events.
